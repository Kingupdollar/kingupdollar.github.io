Lesson 3

Risk Factors of Retinal Detachment

Learning objective: Identify the causes or risk factors of retinal detachment

Skills:

  • list at least three possible risk factors of retinal detachment
  • explain the reason that each risk factor may be a threat to retina

Risk factors for retinal detachment include:

  • severe myopia
  • retinal tears
  • trauma
  • family history
  • complications from cataract surgery

Patients with pathological severe myopia are at increased risk of retinal detachment due to axial elongation of the globe and peripheral retinal thinning. Ophthalmologists generally advise people with high degrees of myopia to try to avoid exposure to activities that have the potential for trauma.

There are many forces inside the eyeball, such as the stickiness of the liquid inside the eyeball, that can lead to retinal tears. If retinal tear happens, that might result in breaks on the retina and let the liquid inside the eyeball to flow into the break and pull the retina out of its original position.

Trauma-related cases of retinal detachment can occur in high-impact sports or in high speed sports. Moreover, increasing pressure on or within the eye itself and rapid acceleration and deceleration (such as bungee jumping or roller coaster rides) are possible risk factors as well.

Genetic factors promoting local inflammation and photoreceptor degeneration may also be involved in the development of the disease.
